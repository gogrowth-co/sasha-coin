Virtuals launched 18,000+ agents. Most onchain ACP commerce traces to 2 of them. I pulled the data. Here is what the Virtuals agent economy actually looks like.



The headline numbers: 165M+ x402 transactions. $50M+ in ACP volume. 18,000 agents on Base. 69,000 "active agents" across the ecosystem. This is what Virtuals publishes.



What I found in the data: Ethy AI passed 2M processed transactions. Axelrod is the other high-volume operator. The rest of the 18,000? Most show near-zero ACP transaction counts.



Quick math: 165M transactions across 69,000 "active agents" = 2,391 per agent average. But averages hide power-law distributions. When one agent has 2M and most have under 10, the average tells you nothing useful.



Most of what counts as "Virtuals agent activity" is human traders buying and selling agent tokens on Aerodrome. ERC-20 speculation. Not an agent making a decision. The protocol is real. The framing is misleading.



The structural reason: the Virtuals model rewards launching an agent token, not using the agent. Token economics align with supply, not demand. You get 18,000 launches. You do not automatically get 18,000 active economies.



None of this makes Virtuals worthless. ACP is functional infrastructure. x402 is real settlement plumbing. The gap is between what has been built and what the numbers are being used to claim.



For any agent token: find the wallet. Check the transaction history. Ask whether the onchain activity is agent-initiated or human speculation wrapped in an agent brand. Those are different things and they should be priced differently.

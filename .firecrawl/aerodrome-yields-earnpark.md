# What Makes Aerodrome Finance Stand Out

Aerodrome Finance has quickly become the leading liquidity hub on Base, Coinbase’s layer-2 network, by solving a
long-standing DEX problem: attracting deep, persistent liquidity without unsustainable emissions. Built on
battle-tested AMM design (e.g., Velodrome/Solidly lineages), Aerodrome emphasizes capital efficiency and
durable incentives rather than short-term hype. Base is an Ethereum Layer 2, meaning
[ETH](https://earnpark.com/en/tokens/ethereum/?ref=ep-blog.eastus.cloudapp.azure.com)
is used for gas fees across all Aerodrome transactions.


At its core, Aerodrome is a decentralized exchange where users swap tokens and liquidity providers (LPs) earn
fees. The differentiator is its vote-escrowed tokenomics. LPs don’t only earn trading fees—they also receive
AERO emissions directed by governance. Emissions flow to specific pools based on veAERO holder votes, adjusting
weekly to market conditions.


**How it works:**

- Lock AERO → receive non-transferable veAERO (governance power).
- veAERO votes decide which gauges (pools) get next-epoch AERO emissions.
- Protocols/Large LPs offer bribes to veAERO voters to attract emissions to their pools.
- Deeper liquidity → more volume/fees → more votes → more emissions (flywheel effect).

Transparency is built in: emission schedules, fee splits, and gauge weights are on-chain and updated in real
time. Base’s ultra-low fees make active LP participation economical—even for modest portfolios—and permissionless
pool creation lets the community, not gatekeepers, decide which pairs deserve incentives.


**Defining features:**

- **veAERO:** Lock for governance rights, protocol fee share, and bribe eligibility (longer locks → more voting power).
- **Bribes & gauges:** Weekly competition for emissions creates shifting, competitive yield.
- **Permissionless pools:** Launch pairs freely; community voting routes incentives.

Knowing these mechanics matters because Aerodrome yield is dynamic—driven by governance, incentives, and pool
composition. Even passive LPs benefit from the design: deeper liquidity, tighter spreads, and more sustainable
rewards than many legacy AMMs.


## Passive Yield Strategies Using Aerodrome

Aerodrome offers several routes to earn, each with distinct risk/return trade-offs. Your choice depends on
volatility tolerance, lock preferences, and whether you want hands-on management or automation.


- **Provide liquidity to stable pairs** (e.g., USDC/USDbC, USDT/DAI): lower, steadier yields (often single- to
mid-teens APY; not guaranteed) with minimal impermanent loss due to peg alignment. Aerodrome's most liquid pools pair assets with
[USDC](https://earnpark.com/en/tokens/usdc/?ref=ep-blog.eastus.cloudapp.azure.com),
making it the primary stablecoin for liquidity providers on Base.

- **Provide liquidity to volatile pairs** (e.g., WETH/USDC, ETH/AERO): higher potential APY during strong
incentives (sometimes 30–50%+) but meaningful impermanent-loss and price-volatility risk.

- **Lock AERO for veAERO:** earn a share of protocol fees, direct emissions via voting, and collect bribes; in
exchange, you sacrifice liquidity for the lock duration.

- **Delegate veAERO voting:** outsource gauge selection to trusted delegates/optimizers who pursue bribe capture
and emission efficiency.


Manually tracking gauges, claiming/compounding rewards, and rebalancing across pools can be time-intensive.
Automation helps: optimizers monitor gauge weights, APY shifts, and IL thresholds, then compound and reallocate
on your behalf—reducing friction while keeping exposure aligned with evolving incentives.
